01 — Philosophy

Your grid is a
curated exhibition,
not a feed.

Shelley, your work already speaks in a distinct visual language — close, tactile, graphic, restrained. It spans luxury beauty campaigns (Armani, Smells), editorial still-life, and a personal practice that moves between the witty and the quietly profound.

The problem with most photographer feeds is incoherence: commission drops next to a personal project with no connective tissue. Clients see a portfolio in chaos. Peers see inconsistency. Followers disengage.

The strategy below treats your grid as a single, sustained argument for your vision. Each post is a sentence. Each week is a paragraph. The alternation between commissioned, editorial, and personal work is the rhythm — and rhythm is what creates desire.

Reference points: Nadège Meriau's obsessive materiality, Corinne Day's intimacy, the anti-heroism of Wolfgang Tillmans, the colour decisions of Viviane Sassen. But always Shelley first.

I
Coherence over frequency
Five considered posts beats seven rushed ones. Quality of selection is the entire strategy.
II
Contrast creates dialogue
Alternating pillars creates visual conversations across the grid. An Armani bottle next to a smiley-face biscuit is unexpectedly brilliant.
III
Captions as whispers
Short, oblique, sometimes silent. Never explanatory. Let the work lead.
IV
The grid is a gallery hang
Before posting, preview the 3×3. Tonal balance — dark/light, tight/wide — matters as much as the individual image.
V
Stories are the studio
The feed is finished work. Stories are process, personality, behind the frame. Keep them distinct.

On Timezone Strategy
Melbourne sits in an unusual position for international reach. The Thursday and Friday evening slots are your best bridge to US and UK markets simultaneously — lean into these for your most commercially relevant work.
Instagram's Algorithm
Instagram now weights early engagement heavily. The first 30 minutes matter most — post, then engage with comments and Stories immediately to signal activity and boost distribution.
Carousel Advantage
Carousels receive up to 3× more reach than single images because Instagram re-serves them to users who didn't swipe. Use carousels for campaign work and series — 3 to 8 images maximum.
Reels as Amplification
One Reel per fortnight — a 15–30 second atmospheric clip from a shoot, no sound or ambient sound only. Reels are distributed beyond your follower base. Think of them as discovery content, not portfolio content.

05 — Caption Voice

The image speaks.
The caption breathes.

⬤  Commissioned
"Shot for @armanibeauty. More soon."
Credit the client first. No gushing. Let the work be the persuasion. Optional: name the product, name a collaborator. Never describe what the audience can see. 1–2 lines maximum. Always tag the brand.
⬤  Editorial
"Tape measure. Blue. 2025."

— or —

"Something about admitting you're lost."
Oblique, spare, curious. Could be the title of the work. Could be the thought that produced it. Never a full explanation. Reference the publication only if it adds something; otherwise omit it. Let the ambiguity work.
⬤  Personal
"Smiley faces are the most honest form of punctuation."

— or —

[no caption]
Complete latitude. Can be a fragment, a note-to-self, a found line, a date. Or nothing at all — silence is valid for personal work. These posts are about relationship, not description. Occasionally longer (3–4 lines) if there's a real thought.

08 — Growth & Engagement Tactics

Beyond the
feed.

◻
Stories: The Studio Layer
Post 3–5 Stories per week independently of feed posts. Process footage, studio snaps, colour references, things that caught your eye. Stories keep you present in the algorithm between feed posts and build the sense of a real practice happening in real time. Use the "Close Friends" list for more personal, unfinished work.
◻
Strategic Tagging
Always tag brands in both the image and caption when posting commissioned work. Tag collaborators (makeup artists, stylists, art directors) — they reshare, extending your reach to adjacent professional audiences. For personal work, tag 1–2 galleries or publications whose aesthetic aligns with yours. Avoid generic "photography" tags.
◻
Engagement: Give Before You Get
Spend 10 minutes after each post engaging with accounts in your peer group — Australian photographers, international creatives, art directors who follow you. Comment with genuine specificity, not emoji. The algorithm reads engagement velocity. Your comments on others' work increase your own distribution.
◻
Bi-monthly Reels
One atmospheric Reel every 2–3 weeks. 15–20 seconds. Moving product, drifting light across a set, hands at work. No text overlays. Ambient sound or silence. Reels are your discovery mechanism — they reach non-followers. Think of them as the trailer for the exhibition the feed represents.
◻
Highlights as Portfolio
Restructure your Highlights to mirror your three content pillars: Commissioned, Editorial, Personal. Add a fourth: Process. This turns your profile into a navigable portfolio for new visitors — especially art directors who land on your page from a tag. Update each Highlight monthly.
◻
Dropbox Review Workflow
Share your Dropbox folder and I'll review your archive week-by-week, selecting and matching specific images to each daily slot based on tonal contrast, pillar fit, and 3×3 grid harmony. We can plan 2–4 weeks ahead so you're never scrambling. This is where the strategy becomes the actual feed.
